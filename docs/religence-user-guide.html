1. Introduction

Religence CRM is a pharmaceutical customer relationship management application designed for API salt and branded medicine outreach. It helps your team discover manufacturers and marketers, qualify prospects, manage an active sales pipeline, send templated emails, track conversations, and measure performance — all from a single dashboard.

Who This Guide Is For

  • Sales representatives managing day-to-day lead follow-up and email outreach
  • Business development managers overseeing pipeline health and team activity
  • CRM administrators maintaining salts, medicines, and email templates

What You Can Do

  • Search for companies by API salt and branded medicine using Lead Discovery
  • Save verified prospects as contacts and active leads
  • Track leads through a structured pipeline from first save to Won/Lost
  • Send personalized emails using merge-field templates
  • Manage inbound and outbound mail in a unified Inbox
  • Review KPIs, pipeline breakdown, and outreach activity in Reports
  • Maintain master lists of salts and medicines under Settings

How This Guide Is Organized

Sections follow the natural workflow: configure master data → discover prospects → save and verify → manage pipeline → communicate → analyze results. Each chapter includes step-by-step instructions, tips for effective use, and notes on how screens connect to each other.

Recommended first-time setup: Configure Salts Master and Medicine Master (Chapter 4), review default Email Templates (Chapter 9), then run your first Lead Discovery session (Chapter 5).

3. Understanding CRM Concepts

3.1 Core Entities

EntityDescription
CompanyA manufacturer, marketer, or exporter saved from discovery (name, location, certification, website)
ContactA person at a company (name, role, email, phone) linked to one company
LeadAn active sales opportunity with stage, score, assignee, follow-up date, and product match
DealA commercial opportunity record created when a lead reaches Won or via Close Deal
EmailInbound or outbound message, optionally linked to a lead
Timeline eventAudit entry for stage changes, emails, notes, verification, and deals

3.2 Salts, Medicines, and Leads

The product hierarchy drives discovery and lead matching:

  • Salt (API) — active pharmaceutical ingredient, e.g. Budesonide
  • Medicine — branded product linked to one salt, e.g. Budecort Respules (Respules)
  • Lead product match — each lead stores matched salt name, medicine name, and dosage form

In Lead Discovery, you select salts → medicines appear → companies matching the active medicine are shown. When saved, this product context flows into the lead record and email templates.

3.3 Lead Pipeline Stages

Every lead progresses through defined stages. Understanding stage order is essential for effective pipeline management.

Saved → Verified → Intro Email Sent → Follow-up Sent → Replied → Sample Requested → Quotation Sent → Negotiation → Won / Lost / Dormant

SavedNew from discovery; not yet approvedVerify in queue or drawer
VerifiedApproved for outreachSend intro email
Intro Email SentFirst outreach deliveredWait or send follow-up
Follow-up SentSecond touch sentMonitor inbox for reply
RepliedProspect respondedQualify interest; request sample
Sample RequestedSample stage activePrepare quotation
Quotation SentCommercial offer sharedNegotiate terms

4. Setting Up Master Data

Before heavy prospecting, review your Salts Master and Medicine Master. These catalogs power Lead Discovery filtering and appear on lead records throughout the CRM.

4.1 Salts Master (/settings/salts)

Layout: Paginated table (left) + editor (right)

Table columns

  • # — row number
  • Salt name — API name (click row to select)
  • Meds — count of linked medicines
  • Type — Built-in (default 18 salts) or Custom

Actions

  • Search — filter salts by name
  • New salt — add a custom salt (opens in editor as "Untitled salt")
  • Save changes — persist name edits (updates matched salt on linked leads)
  • Reset — restore built-in salt to default name
  • Delete — remove custom salt (only if no medicines or leads reference it)
  • Reset all — restore entire default salt list
  • Lead Discovery — jump to discovery with current catalog
Religence CRM User Guide8

4.2 Medicine Master (/settings/medicines)

Medicines are branded products mapped to a salt. Each medicine has a name, linked salt, and dosage form.

Table columns

  • Medicine — branded product name
  • Salt — parent API (shown on wider screens)
  • Form — dosage form badge (Tablet, Respules, Inhaler, etc.)
  • Type — Built-in or Custom

Editor fields

  • Medicine name — e.g. Budecort Respules
  • Linked salt — dropdown of all salts in Salts Master
  • Dosage form — Tablet, Capsule, Syrup, Injection, Inhaler, Respules, Cream, Ointment, Nasal Spray, Drops

Rules

  • Built-in medicines (26 defaults) can be edited and reset but not deleted
  • Custom medicines can be deleted only when no active leads reference the medicine name
  • Renaming a medicine updates matchedMedicine on all linked leads
Admin tip: Add custom salts first, then medicines linked to those salts. New entries appear in Lead Discovery immediately after save — no restart required.
Religence CRM User Guide9

4.3 Master Data Best Practices

Do

  • Use consistent salt naming (match pharmacopoeia names)
  • Link every medicine to the correct salt before discovery
  • Review built-in defaults before adding duplicates
  • Use Custom badge entries for new product lines

Avoid

  • Deleting salts that still have medicines linked
  • Renaming salts/medicines without checking lead reports
  • Using Reset all during active campaigns
  • Creating duplicate medicine names under different salts

4.4 Data Persistence

Salts, medicines, templates, leads, contacts, emails, and all CRM records are saved automatically to your browser's local storage (key: religence-crm-v1). Changes persist across sessions on the same browser and device. See Chapter 14 for backup and reset considerations.

5. Lead Discovery

Lead Discovery (/lead-discovery) is a three-panel prospecting workspace for finding companies by API salt and branded medicine.

5.1 Panel Layout

PanelPositionFunction
SaltsLeftCheckbox list of all salts; search and pagination
MedicinesCenterMedicines for checked salts; set one as active
ResultsRightCompanies matching the active medicine

5.2 Step-by-Step Discovery

  1. Open Lead Discovery from the sidebar.
  2. In the Salts panel, check one or more salts relevant to your campaign.
  3. The Medicines panel auto-populates with linked products. All medicines are selected by default when salts change.
  4. Click a medicine row to set it as active (highlighted). The Results panel updates.
  5. Review companies: name, type (Manufacturer/Marketer/Exporter), location, and lead score.
  6. Click a company name to open the Company Profile drawer.
Religence CRM User Guide11

5.3 Company Profile Drawer

The profile drawer provides deep context before saving a prospect to CRM.

Tabs

  • Overview — company narrative, matched salt/medicine/dosage, website, AI summary, match reasons
  • Products — product list associated with the match
  • Contacts — known contacts with role, email, and phone
  • Certifications — WHO-GMP, ISO, and other badges
  • Source Proof — external reference links
  • AI Notes — automated research notes

Status indicators

  • Saved to CRM — company already exists; may link to existing lead
  • Save to Contact — primary button to add to CRM (see Chapter 6)

5.4 Discovery Tips

  • Start with one salt when learning the interface; expand to multi-salt searches for portfolio campaigns
  • Sort mentally by lead score in Results — higher scores indicate stronger matches
  • Use pagination in Salts/Medicines panels for large catalogs
  • Re-check master data if expected medicines do not appear after selecting a salt

6. Saving Prospects to CRM

When you find a promising company in Lead Discovery, use Save to Contact from the Company Profile drawer.

6.1 Save Options

OptionCreatesBest For
Save company onlyCompany recordResearch phase; no contact yet
Save company + contactCompany + contactKnown contact, no active deal
Save and create active leadCompany + contact + lead (Saved stage)Standard pipeline entry
Save and start email outreachSame as above + opens Send EmailReady-to-contact prospects

6.2 Contact Selection

If the company profile lists multiple contacts, choose the Primary contact from the dropdown before saving. The selected contact's email and role flow into the lead record.

6.3 After Saving

  • Company only / + contact: Toast confirmation; remain on discovery or visit Saved Contact
  • Create lead: Redirects to Active Leads with lead drawer open (?lead=)
  • Start outreach: Redirects to Active Leads with Send Email modal (?compose=)
If the company is Already in CRM, saving updates existing records rather than creating duplicates. Check the status badge in the profile drawer before saving.
Religence CRM User Guide14

6.4 What Gets Created

When you save with a lead, Religence creates interconnected records:

  • Lead title — formatted as {medicine} — {company}
  • Product match — salt name, medicine name, and dosage form from discovery context
  • Stage — starts at Saved (requires verification before outreach in strict workflows)
  • Lead score — carried from discovery company score
  • Source links — proof URLs from the company profile
  • Timeline — initial event logged for audit trail

6.5 Effective Save Strategy

  1. Review company profile thoroughly (certifications, contacts, source proof)
  2. Save as lead (not company-only) when you intend to follow up within 7 days
  3. Use "Start email outreach" only after verification if your team requires it
  4. Assign the lead to the correct rep in Active Leads after save

7. Verification Queue

The Verification Queue (/verification-queue) lists all leads at the Saved stage awaiting approval before outreach.

7.1 When to Use

Teams with a qualification gate use this screen daily. Managers review new discovery saves for data quality and strategic fit before reps send emails.

7.2 Screen Elements

  • Pending count badge — number of Saved leads
  • Table columns: Lead, Company, Salt/Medicine, Stage, Score, Actions
  • Verify — moves lead to Verified and logs timeline event
  • Review — opens lead in Active Leads drawer
  • Source — returns to Lead Discovery

7.3 Alternative Verification Path

You can also verify from the Active Leads detail drawer: when stage is Saved, a Verify button appears in the stage bar. Both paths produce the same result.

Bookmark /verification-queue — this route is not in the sidebar. Consider adding it to team onboarding materials.

8. Active Leads Pipeline

Active Leads (/active-leads) is your daily command center for managing the sales pipeline.

8.1 Dashboard KPIs

Four stat cards at the top summarize pipeline health:

  • Total leads — all leads in CRM
  • In pipeline — non-terminal stages
  • Closed — Won, Lost, and Dormant combined
  • Follow-up due — leads with follow-up in the next 3 days

8.2 Filtering & Views

  • Pipeline tabs: All | Pipeline | Closed
  • Search: lead title, company, contact, salt
  • Filters: Stage, Salt, Assignee — use Clear to reset
  • Stage chips: quick-filter by stage with live counts

8.3 Lead Table

Columns include avatar, lead & company, contact & assignee, product match (salt/medicine/dosage), stage badge, score, follow-up date. Click any row to open the detail drawer.

Religence CRM User Guide17

8.4 Lead Detail Drawer

Stage bar

  • Stage dropdown — manually set any stage
  • Verify — visible when stage is Saved
  • Advance — move to next pipeline stage
  • Dormant / Lost — mark terminal inactive states

Tabs

  • Overview — company, contact (mailto link), product match, source links, deals, dates
  • Timeline — chronological audit of all activities
  • Emails — thread history; Send email button
  • Notes — free-text notes; auto-save on blur or explicit Save

Footer actions

  • Send email — opens template-based compose modal
  • Close deal — creates deal record when in Negotiation

8.5 Creating Leads Manually

Click New lead to open the creation modal. Requires an existing company (from discovery save). Fill title, optional contact, salt/medicine/dosage, stage, and assignee. Useful for referrals or trade-show contacts not found in discovery.

9. Email Templates & Outreach

Email Templates (/templates) manage merge-field templates used when sending from Active Leads and Inbox.

9.1 Template Library

The left panel lists all templates with category badges:

  • Introduction — first outreach (e.g. First Introduction, Salt-specific Outreach)
  • Follow-up — second touch after no reply
  • Quotation — post-quotation reminders

9.2 Editing Templates

Select a template to edit in the right panel:

  • Template name and Description
  • Category — Introduction, Follow-up, or Quotation
  • Subject and Body with merge field support

9.3 Merge Fields

FieldInserts
{{company_name}}Lead's company name
{{contact_name}}Primary contact name
{{salt_name}}Matched API salt
{{medicine_name}}Matched medicine
{{dosage_form}}Dosage form
{{sender_name}}Current user (Priya Sharma by default)
Religence CRM User Guide20

9.4 Using Merge Fields

  1. Click a merge field chip to insert at cursor position
  2. Use Insert here toggle to target Subject vs Body
  3. Switch to Preview tab to see rendered output
  4. Select Sample data or a real lead for preview context

9.5 Template Actions

  • New template — create custom template
  • Save changes — persist edits (Unsaved badge when dirty)
  • Reset — restore built-in template to default
  • Delete — remove custom templates only
  • Reset all — restore all four default templates

9.6 Sending Email from Active Leads

  1. Open lead drawer → Send email
  2. Select template from dropdown (auto-fills subject and body)
  3. Edit message if needed
  4. If Gmail not connected, click Connect Gmail first
  5. Click Send & update stage

Sending logs the email to Inbox and lead thread, advances pipeline stage, sets follow-up +5 days, and adds a timeline event.

Religence CRM User Guide21

9.7 Outreach Best Practices

  • Always preview templates with a real lead before bulk sends
  • Customize salt-specific outreach for high-value API campaigns
  • Match template category to lead stage (intro vs follow-up vs quotation)
  • Keep subject lines under 60 characters for mobile readability
  • Include a clear call-to-action (call, sample request, portfolio share)
Template hygiene: Review templates quarterly. Update sender name, certification mentions, and product names when your portfolio changes. Use Reset on built-ins if edits go off-track.

9.8 Gmail Connection (Demo Mode)

Religence uses a simulated Gmail connection for demonstration. Clicking Connect Gmail sets the connected flag and enables compose/send. In production deployment, this would integrate with Google OAuth. Sent emails appear in Inbox (Sent folder) and on the lead's Emails tab.

10. Inbox & Email Management

The Inbox (/inbox) provides a four-column email workspace connected to CRM leads.

10.1 Layout

  1. Folder sidebar — Compose, profile, folders, labels, contacts
  2. Message list — search, star, checkboxes, folder filtering
  3. Reading pane — full message with actions
  4. Contacts rail — quick compose to saved contacts

10.2 Folders

All Mails, Inbox (unread count), Sent, Drafts, Spam, Important/Starred, Trash, Archive. Folder counts update from CRM email records and session filters.

10.3 Connecting Gmail

Click Connect Gmail in the Inbox sidebar before composing. Profile shows sales@religence.example.com when connected. Demo mode injects sample inbound messages on first connect.

Religence CRM User Guide23

10.4 Message Actions

  • Link to lead — associate unlinked inbound email with a lead (can advance stage to Replied)
  • Open lead — navigate to linked lead when leadId is set
  • Reply — send response via CRM (updates lead stage like pipeline send)
  • Star, Archive, Delete — organize messages (session state; see note below)

10.5 Compose

Click Compose Mail (requires Gmail connected). Enter To, Subject, Body. Sent messages appear in Sent folder and log to CRM if linked to a lead.

10.6 Labels

Filter by tags: finance (Mall), internal (Home), lead (Work), unlinked (Friends). Useful for triaging mixed inbound mail.

Session-only state: Starred, read/unread, archived, and trashed flags reset on browser refresh. CRM-persisted emails (subject, body, lead link) remain in local storage.

13. Daily Workflow & Best Practices

13.1 Recommended Daily Flow

  1. Morning (15 min): Open Reports → Follow-up Tasks; work overdue items in Active Leads
  2. Mid-morning (30 min): Process Inbox replies; link unlinked messages; advance Replied leads
  3. Prospecting block (45 min): Lead Discovery for target salt → save 3–5 qualified companies
  4. Outreach block (30 min): Send templated emails to Verified leads; update stages
  5. End of day (10 min): Update notes, set follow-up dates, clear Verification Queue items

13.2 Team Roles

RolePrimary ScreensKey Actions
Sales RepActive Leads, Inbox, DiscoveryFollow-up, email, save prospects
BD ManagerReports, Verification QueueApprove leads, review pipeline
AdminSalts/Medicine Master, TemplatesMaintain catalogs and templates

13.3 Data Quality Rules

  • One lead per company-medicine combination unless strategically justified
  • Always verify Saved leads before first email in gated workflows
  • Keep product match fields accurate — they drive templates and reports
  • Log every significant call or meeting in lead notes

All CRM entities persist under localStorage key religence-crm-v1. Theme preferences use separate keys (ynex*). The theme switcher's "Reset all" may wipe CRM data — use with caution.

14.3 Browser Recommendations

Use Chrome or Edge for best compatibility. Keep one primary browser/device for CRM work to avoid split data. For team deployments, plan migration to a shared backend before multi-user production use.
